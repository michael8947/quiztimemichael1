Donald Trump: A Timeline

1946
Born on June 14 in Queens, New York – a humble beginning for a future icon of controversy.
1968
Graduates from the Wharton School with an economics degree. Who knew a love for money started in college?
1971
Takes over his father's real estate empire and rebrands it as The Trump Organization – enter the world of gilded towers and even gildier ambitions.
1980s-1990s
Expands into luxury real estate by building Trump Tower and opening casinos, while also enduring a few financial meltdowns. Even moguls have their messy moments.
2004
Becomes a reality TV star as the host of The Apprentice, turning “You’re fired!” into a catchphrase that echoes through boardrooms and Twitter feeds.
2015
Announces his candidacy for U.S. president as a Republican on an “America First” platform – the political stage just got its own reality show.
2016
Wins the U.S. presidential election against Hillary Clinton – a victory that left pundits and memes scrambling.
2017-2021
Serves as the 45th president. Over these turbulent years, he passes major tax cuts, appoints three conservative Supreme Court justices, and implements strict immigration policies. In 2020, he loses re-election amid controversy, and in early 2021, his supporters storm the Capitol leading to a historic second impeachment (from which he is acquitted). Chaos and executive orders, all in one term.
2022-2023
Faces multiple legal investigations yet remains a key figure in the Republican Party – because drama never really leaves the stage.
2024
Announces another presidential run, aiming for a political comeback. The saga continues – grab your popcorn.
